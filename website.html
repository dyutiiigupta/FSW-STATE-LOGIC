About Us

Who We Are

Northern Light Explorer is a platform dedicated to helping people discover and appreciate the beauty of the Aurora Borealis. We bring together useful information and travel inspiration in one place.

Our Vision

We aim to make Northern Lights exploration simple, enjoyable, and accessible for everyone, from first-time travelers to experienced adventurers.

Our Mission

Our mission is to educate, inspire, and guide people while promoting responsible tourism and respect for Arctic environments.

Our Values

We are committed to providing accurate, up-to-date information and fostering a community of Northern Lights enthusiasts who share a passion for this natural phenomenon.

What Makes Us Different

We combine reliable information, beautiful visuals, and an easy-to-use interface so visitors can plan their Northern Lights journey with confidence.

Our Inspiration

The beauty and mystery of the Aurora Borealis inspire everything we create. We believe this extraordinary natural phenomenon deserves to be explored, appreciated, and shared with people around the world.

Destinations

Tromso, Norway

Best Time: September – March
Known as the "Gateway to the Arctic," Tromsø is one of the world's most reliable destinations for viewing the Northern Lights. Surrounded by snowy mountains and fjords, it offers spectacular winter scenery and a vibrant Arctic culture.
Highlights:
Excellent aurora visibility
Dog sledding & reindeer safaris
Fjord cruises and cable car rides

Abisko, Sweden

Best Time: November – March
Abisko is famous for its clear skies due to the unique "Blue Hole" weather phenomenon, making it one of the best places to witness the Aurora Borealis. The peaceful national park is perfect for nature lovers.
Highlights:
Exceptionally clear skies
Abisko National Park
Snowshoeing and ice climbing

Reykjavik, Iceland

Best Time: September – April
Iceland's capital is an ideal base for Northern Lights adventures. Visitors can combine aurora hunting with breathtaking waterfalls, glaciers, volcanic landscapes, and geothermal hot springs.
Highlights:
Golden Circle tours
Blue Lagoon hot springs
Waterfalls and glaciers

Rovaniemi, Finland

Best Time: September – March
Located on the Arctic Circle, Rovaniemi is the official hometown of Santa Claus. It is famous for its magical snowy landscapes, glass igloos, and unforgettable Northern Lights experiences.
Highlights:
Glass igloo stays
Santa Claus Village
Husky and reindeer safaris

Fairbanks, Alaska

Best Time: August – April
Fairbanks lies directly beneath the Auroral Oval, giving visitors an excellent chance to witness vibrant Northern Lights displays. Its long winter nights make it one of North America's top aurora destinations.
Highlights:
High aurora activity
Chena Hot Springs
Ice museums and wildlife tours

Yellowknife, Canada

Best Time: November – March
Yellowknife is renowned for its clear skies and high aurora activity. The city offers a unique blend of Indigenous culture, winter sports, and unforgettable aurora viewing experiences.
Highlights:
Clear skies & high aurora activity
Indigenous cultural experiences
Ice fishing and snowmobiling

Aurora Viewing Guide

Aurora Activity

  1. KP Index: 4
  2. Visibility: Good
  3. Best Time: 10:00 PM - 2:00 AM
  4. Status: High chance of seeing the Northern Lights

Weather Conditions

  1. Temperature: -8°C
  2. Cloud Cover: 20%
  3. Wind Speed: 12 km/h
  4. Humidity: 70%

Moon Phase

  1. Waning Crescent
  2. Low moonlight provides dark skies.
  3. Better conditions for aurora viewing

Best Viewing Hours

  1. Start: 9:45 PM
  2. Peak Activity: 11:30 PM-1:30 AM
  3. End: 3:00 AM

Photography Conditions

  1. Use a tripod.
  2. ISO: 800-1600
  3. Exposure: 5-15 seconds
  4. Manual focus for sharp stars.

Viewing Advice

  1. Wear warm clothing.
  2. Choose a location away from city lights.
  3. Check the sky for clouds.
  4. Arrive atleast 30 minutes before the predicted peak time.
